PROGRAM ITINERARY

Students will be recruited during the Spring semester. Nine-weeks classroom and field trainings will be conducted during the Summer ending with an open activity where students will be able to share their learning experiences with academics, government representatives and people from their communities. As part of the training hands-on experience, students will have the opportunity to participate in a water quality oceanographic cruise to test their DIY designs along a pre-determined bio-optical transect through Case 1 [oligotrophic] and Case 2 [eutrophic] waters during a Landsat 8 overpass in southwest PR. This study will also leverage several ongoing internship pilots in Puerto Rico. Each year several participants will be selected to participate in the Society for the Advancement of Chicanos/Hispanics and Native Americans in Science (SACNAS) conference.
